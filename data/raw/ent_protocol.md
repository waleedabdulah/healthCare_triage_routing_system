# ENT (Ear, Nose & Throat) Symptom Protocol

## EMERGENCY — Route to ER
- Severe difficulty swallowing with drooling and inability to open mouth (possible abscess)
- Foreign object inhaled or stuck in throat causing breathing difficulty
- Severe nosebleed that cannot be controlled after 20 minutes of direct pressure
- Throat swelling causing breathing difficulty (airway compromise)
- Ear injury with clear fluid from ear canal after head trauma

## URGENT — Same-day ENT or OPD
- Severe sore throat with difficulty swallowing, fever >38.5°C (possible tonsillitis/strep)
- Suspected ear infection with severe pain and fever in child
- Sudden hearing loss (one ear, complete, new onset)
- Severe sinus pain with fever and facial swelling
- Peritonsillar swelling (one side of throat more swollen) — possible abscess
- Nosebleed with clots, moderate bleeding, recurring daily

## NON-URGENT — Routine ENT OPD
- Mild to moderate sore throat without severe difficulty swallowing
- Chronic ear pain without fever
- Gradual hearing loss or ringing in ears (tinnitus)
- Chronic sinus congestion or post-nasal drip
- Mild recurrent nosebleeds (controlled quickly)
- Voice hoarseness lasting >2 weeks
- Difficulty hearing in one ear (gradual)

## SELF-CARE
- Minor sore throat with no fever, no difficulty swallowing
- Common cold with nasal congestion
- Mild ear discomfort after swimming

## Key Assessment Questions
- Is there difficulty breathing due to the throat problem?
- Can the patient swallow their own saliva?
- Is there a fever?
- Did hearing loss come on suddenly or gradually?

## Routing
→ Primary Department: ENT (Ear, Nose & Throat)
→ Emergency Override: ER if airway compromised, inability to swallow, uncontrolled bleeding
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (airway) / URGENT (fever+throat, sudden hearing loss) / NON_URGENT (mild, chronic)
