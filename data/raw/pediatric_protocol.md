# Pediatric Symptom Protocol (Children Under 16)

## EMERGENCY — Route to ER
- Infant (<3 months) with any fever ≥38°C (100.4°F)
- Child with fever + stiff neck + light sensitivity + rash (meningitis signs)
- Child with seizure (first seizure, or seizure lasting >5 minutes)
- Infant not responding, limp, or unresponsive
- Child with severe breathing difficulty or choking
- Child with severe allergic reaction (throat swelling, trouble breathing)
- Child with signs of dehydration: no tears, no urine for 8+ hours, sunken eyes
- Child with suspected poisoning or accidental ingestion

## URGENT — Same-day Pediatrics OPD
- Fever >39°C (102.2°F) in child 3 months–5 years with no clear source
- Fever lasting more than 3 days in school-age child
- Child with moderate breathing difficulty (fast breathing but not severe)
- Ear pain with fever in child (suspected otitis media)
- Child with severe sore throat and difficulty swallowing
- Child with rash + fever (needs evaluation)
- Infant with persistent crying (more than 2 hours, inconsolable)
- Child with vomiting + diarrhea and signs of mild dehydration

## NON-URGENT — Routine Pediatrics OPD
- Mild cold, runny nose, mild cough in child over 3 months
- Low fever (<38.5°C) in child over 3 months, alert and active
- Minor cuts, scrapes without infection signs
- Growth or development concerns (scheduled review)
- Vaccination visit
- Mild stomach ache without vomiting

## Age-Adjusted Urgency Rules
- <3 months: any fever = EMERGENCY
- 3–6 months: fever ≥38.5°C = URGENT
- 6 months–2 years: fever ≥39°C with no source = URGENT
- >2 years: fever alone not alarming unless >39.5°C or lasting >3 days
- High fever (>40°C/104°F) in any age child = URGENT

## Routing
→ Primary Department: Pediatrics
→ Secondary: Relevant specialty if symptom-specific (e.g., ENT for ear infection, Ortho for injury)
→ Emergency Override: ER if infant fever, seizure, severe breathing, poisoning, severe dehydration
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (infant fever, seizure, not responding) / URGENT (high fever, ear infection) / NON_URGENT (mild symptoms, normal child)
