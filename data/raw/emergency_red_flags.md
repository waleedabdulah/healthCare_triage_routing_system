# Emergency Red Flags — Route to ER Immediately

These symptom patterns require IMMEDIATE emergency care. Do not wait. Do not attempt OPD.

## Cardiac Red Flags
- Chest pain, chest pressure, or chest tightness (especially with sweating, arm pain, jaw pain)
- Chest pain radiating to left arm, shoulder, neck, or jaw
- Sudden severe chest pain described as "crushing" or "squeezing"
- Irregular or very rapid heartbeat with dizziness or fainting

## Breathing Emergencies
- Severe shortness of breath or inability to complete a sentence
- Breathing stopped or very labored
- Lips or fingernails turning blue (cyanosis)
- Choking, airway obstruction
- Severe asthma attack not responding to inhaler

## Neurological Emergencies (Stroke — use FAST test)
- Face drooping on one side
- Arm weakness or numbness suddenly on one side
- Speech difficulty: slurred, confused, or unable to speak
- Sudden severe headache ("worst headache of my life")
- Sudden loss of vision in one or both eyes
- Sudden loss of balance, coordination, or walking ability
- Seizures or convulsions — especially first seizure or prolonged seizure

## Bleeding Emergencies
- Severe uncontrolled bleeding that does not stop with direct pressure
- Vomiting blood or coughing up blood
- Blood in stool (large amount, bright red or black tarry)
- Severe internal injury suspected

## Loss of Consciousness
- Patient is unconscious or unresponsive
- Fainting with no recovery within seconds
- Altered mental status: severe confusion, disorientation

## Trauma
- Severe head injury (vehicle accident, fall from height)
- Suspected spinal injury — do not move the patient
- Penetrating injury (stab, gunshot)
- Crush injury
- Severe burns covering large body area

## Allergic Reaction / Anaphylaxis
- Throat swelling or closing
- Tongue swelling
- Hives with difficulty breathing
- Severe allergic reaction after bee sting, food, or medication

## Other Emergencies
- Suspected poisoning or drug overdose
- Diabetic emergency: unresponsive with known diabetes, severe hypoglycemia
- Severe dehydration with confusion
- High fever (>40°C / 104°F) with stiff neck and light sensitivity (meningitis signs)
- Pregnancy emergencies: heavy bleeding, severe abdominal pain in pregnancy

## Routing
→ Department: Emergency Room
→ Action: GO IMMEDIATELY. Call emergency services if unable to transport.
→ Wait Time: Immediate — ER staff attend on arrival
