# Chest Pain & Cardiac Symptom Protocol

## Overview
Chest pain requires careful urgency assessment. Always err on the side of EMERGENCY when in doubt.

## EMERGENCY Indicators — Route to ER
- Chest pain + sweating (diaphoresis)
- Chest pain radiating to left arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Chest pain + shortness of breath
- Chest pain + nausea or vomiting
- Chest pain described as "crushing," "squeezing," or "pressure"
- Chest pain + palpitations + dizziness
- Severe chest pain of sudden onset (>7/10 severity)
- Any chest pain in patient with known heart disease

## URGENT Indicators — Same-day Cardiology
- Moderate chest pain without radiation, duration <24 hours
- Palpitations (heart racing/skipping) without chest pain
- Newly elevated blood pressure with mild symptoms
- Chest discomfort with mild exertion only
- Prior cardiac history with new mild symptoms

## NON-URGENT — Routine Cardiology OPD
- Mild palpitations, occasional, not worsening
- Known hypertension, stable, routine follow-up
- Family history of heart disease, preventive consultation
- Mild chest wall pain (musculoskeletal — worse with pressure/movement)

## Key Differentiators
- Musculoskeletal chest pain: worse when pressing on chest wall, worse with movement
- Cardiac chest pain: unrelated to touch, may radiate, associated with sweating
- GI chest pain: burning sensation, related to meals, relieved by antacids

## Routing
→ Primary Department: Cardiology
→ Emergency Override: ER if any red flag present
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (with red flags) / URGENT (without red flags but concerning) / NON_URGENT (stable, mild)
