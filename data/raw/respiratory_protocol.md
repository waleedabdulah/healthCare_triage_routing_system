# Respiratory Symptom Protocol

## EMERGENCY — Route to ER
- Severe shortness of breath at rest — cannot speak in full sentences
- Breathing stopped or near-stopped
- Lips or fingernails turning blue (cyanosis)
- Severe asthma attack not responding to reliever inhaler
- Choking or foreign body airway obstruction
- Coughing up large amounts of blood

## URGENT — Same-day Pulmonology or OPD
- Moderate shortness of breath — walking short distances causes distress
- Wheezing with mild-moderate difficulty breathing
- Fever + productive cough + chest pain (possible pneumonia)
- Rapid breathing (more than 25 breaths/minute in adult)
- Coughing up blood (small amount, first time)
- Known asthmatic with increasing frequency of attacks
- Severe cough with vomiting

## NON-URGENT — Routine Pulmonology OPD
- Chronic cough lasting >3 weeks without acute distress
- Mild persistent cough, no fever, no breathing difficulty
- Known COPD or asthma, stable, routine review
- Cough with mild cold symptoms and normal breathing
- Mild chest congestion without fever

## SELF-CARE
- Mild cough with normal breathing
- Common cold with minor cough
- Dry throat causing cough, no fever

## Proxy Severity Assessment (when SpO2 unavailable)
- Can speak in full sentences without stopping = mild
- Must pause mid-sentence to breathe = moderate → URGENT
- Cannot speak or whisper = severe → EMERGENCY

## Routing
→ Primary Department: Pulmonology
→ Emergency Override: ER if cannot complete sentences, cyanosis, or severe attack
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (severe) / URGENT (moderate, fever+cough) / NON_URGENT (mild, chronic stable)
