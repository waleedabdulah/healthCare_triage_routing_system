# Orthopedic Symptom Protocol

## EMERGENCY — Route to ER
- Open fracture (bone visible through skin)
- Suspected spinal injury — do not move the patient, call emergency
- Limb deformity with neurovascular compromise (no pulse, cold, pale, numb limb below injury)
- Severe trauma: vehicle accident, fall from significant height
- Dislocation of major joint (hip, knee, shoulder) with neurovascular signs
- Compartment syndrome signs: severe pain, tightness, numbness in injured limb

## URGENT — Same-day Orthopedics or OPD
- Suspected fracture (significant swelling, deformity, inability to bear weight)
- Joint dislocation without neurovascular compromise
- Severe sprain — unable to bear weight or use limb
- Acute back injury with leg numbness or weakness (possible nerve compression)
- Severe joint pain after acute injury (mechanism of injury + swelling)
- Sports injury with significant swelling and function loss

## NON-URGENT — Routine Orthopedics OPD
- Chronic joint pain (knee, hip, shoulder) — osteoarthritis, stable
- Back pain, chronic, no neurological symptoms
- Minor sprain with ability to bear weight
- Tendon or ligament soreness from overuse
- Mild swelling in joint without clear injury
- Post-surgical follow-up

## SELF-CARE
- Minor muscle soreness after exercise (DOMS)
- Minor bruising without swelling or deformity
- Mild back stiffness in the morning, improving during the day

## Key Assessment Questions
- Was there a trauma or fall?
- Can the patient bear weight on the injured limb?
- Is there deformity, significant swelling, or bruising?
- Is there any numbness or tingling below the injury?
- Is the limb cold or discolored?

## Routing
→ Primary Department: Orthopedics
→ Emergency Override: ER if open fracture, spinal injury, neurovascular compromise
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (open fracture, spinal, vascular) / URGENT (fracture suspected, severe) / NON_URGENT (chronic, stable)
