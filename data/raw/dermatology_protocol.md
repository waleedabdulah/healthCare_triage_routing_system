# Dermatology Symptom Protocol

## EMERGENCY — Route to ER
- Rash + difficulty breathing or throat swelling (anaphylaxis)
- Spreading red skin with fever + confusion (possible sepsis from skin infection)
- Rapidly spreading painful red skin with blistering (necrotizing fasciitis signs)
- Burns covering large body area
- Severe allergic reaction affecting whole body

## URGENT — Same-day Dermatology or OPD
- Infected wound with spreading redness, warmth, pus, or fever
- Rash + fever (especially in children) — needs evaluation
- Sudden widespread hives (urticaria) without breathing difficulty
- Animal bite or human bite with skin break
- Rapidly spreading rash on face, spreading to neck
- Severely itchy rash interfering with sleep

## NON-URGENT — Routine Dermatology OPD
- Acne (mild to moderate, no infection signs)
- Eczema or psoriasis flare-up (stable, known condition)
- Dry, flaky, or itchy skin patches (chronic)
- Suspicious mole or skin lesion — routine evaluation
- Hair loss
- Nail problems
- Mild fungal infections (ringworm, athlete's foot)
- Minor wounds healing poorly

## SELF-CARE
- Mild insect bite with local redness only
- Minor rash from known contact (e.g., plant contact) with no spreading
- Mild dry skin

## Key Assessment Questions
- Is there fever with the rash?
- Is the rash spreading rapidly?
- Is there any difficulty breathing or throat tightness?
- Is there warmth, pus, or tenderness at the site?

## Routing
→ Primary Department: Dermatology
→ Emergency Override: ER if rash + breathing difficulty (anaphylaxis) or rapidly spreading with fever
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (anaphylaxis) / URGENT (infection, fever+rash) / NON_URGENT (chronic, stable)
