# Self-Care Guide — Monitor at Home

## When Self-Care Is Appropriate
These symptoms can typically be managed at home with rest and basic care.
**However: visit OPD if symptoms worsen, persist beyond 5–7 days, or new symptoms appear.**

## Common Cold
- Runny nose, mild congestion, sneezing
- Mild sore throat (no difficulty swallowing)
- Low-grade fever (<38°C)
- Mild body aches
→ Rest, stay hydrated, warm fluids

## Mild Headache
- Tension-type headache, familiar to patient
- No vision changes, no neck stiffness, no sudden onset
- Responds to rest or standard pain relief
→ Rest, hydration, avoid bright screens

## Mild Fatigue
- General tiredness after exertion or poor sleep
- No fever, no other symptoms
→ Rest, adequate sleep, hydration

## Minor Digestive Upset
- Mild stomach discomfort after eating
- Gas or bloating without pain
- Once or twice loose stool without fever or blood
→ Light diet, stay hydrated, rest

## Minor Skin Irritation
- Mild insect bite with local itch only
- Minor rash from known contact (no spreading, no fever)
→ Avoid scratching, apply cool compress

## Mild Muscle Soreness
- Soreness after exercise (DOMS — delayed onset muscle soreness)
- No injury mechanism, no swelling
→ Rest, gentle stretching, warm compress

## When to Seek OPD Care
Upgrade to OPD visit if:
- Fever rises above 38.5°C or lasts more than 3 days
- Symptoms worsen rather than improve
- New symptoms appear (breathing difficulty, rash, severe pain)
- You are elderly, immunocompromised, or have chronic conditions
- Symptoms persist beyond 7 days

## Routing
→ Action: Monitor at home
→ Optional: Schedule OPD appointment if symptoms persist
