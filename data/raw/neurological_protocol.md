# Neurological Symptom Protocol

## EMERGENCY — Route to ER Immediately
- Sudden severe headache ("worst headache of my life") — possible subarachnoid hemorrhage
- Stroke signs (FAST): Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency
- First-ever seizure or prolonged seizure (>5 minutes)
- Sudden loss of vision in one or both eyes
- Sudden loss of balance or coordination
- Sudden confusion or altered consciousness
- Headache with stiff neck + fever + light sensitivity — possible meningitis
- Headache after head injury

## URGENT — Same-day Neurology
- Severe migraine not responding to usual medication
- New onset weakness or numbness in limbs (gradual onset, hours)
- Dizziness with vomiting and inability to walk
- Recurrent seizures in known epileptic patient
- Sudden hearing loss (one side)
- Transient vision changes with headache

## NON-URGENT — Routine Neurology OPD
- Chronic recurring headache, stable pattern
- Mild intermittent dizziness without vomiting
- Known epilepsy, stable on medication, routine review
- Mild tingling or numbness in hands/feet
- Memory concerns without acute changes
- Sleep disorders, tremors (non-acute)

## Key Assessment Questions
- Did symptoms come on suddenly or gradually?
- Is the headache the "worst ever" or different from usual?
- Any weakness, numbness, or vision changes associated?
- Any recent head injury or trauma?

## Routing
→ Primary Department: Neurology
→ Emergency Override: ER if stroke signs, sudden worst headache, loss of consciousness, or seizure
→ Urgency: EMERGENCY (acute neurological signs) / URGENT (new symptoms, moderate) / NON_URGENT (chronic, stable)
